Neuro Bytes
Where does our agency end and the algorithm take over?
Components to the project ▼
  • ★ Fully edited 25-second video.
    • ➜ Equipped with VFX, 3D animation, and immersive film techniques.
  • ★ Interactive control panel.
  • ★ Meditative AE simulations.
  • ★ Virtual gallery.
Concept ▼
Technological embodiment, immersion, posthumanism, cybernetic loops

.

This project centers around a short video that explores how human cognition and digital technology intertwine, illustrating a cycle of productivity, burnout, and digital embodiment. Influenced by posthumanist thought, the project blurs the boundary between human agency and algorithmic influence, showing how technology extends and distorts self-perception.

Glitches, corrupted data, and intrusive notifications highlight the tension between human cognition and technological acceleration. The protagonist’s interface, layered with health metrics, emotional tracking, and a Tamagotchi-style wellness monitor, turns the body into an informatic system, always optimizing yet bound to fail. Borrowing from PC Music aesthetics and early internet kitsch, neon UI elements lend a hyper-stylized lens on digital embodiment.

A diegetic control panel reflects the protagonist’s deteriorating mental state, with polished notifications interrupting at moments of peak overload, both narrative cues and commentary on the gamification of digital labor. As the protagonist snaps, the system plunges into glitch chaos, ending in a mutual burnout of mind and machine. The video is not only reflective of technological discourse but overall self-medication and addiction.

Through experimental effects, UI-driven storytelling, and reflections on digital subjectivity, “Neuro Bytes” probes the pressures of technological embodiment, revealing how humans and machines continually shape each other in an era of algorithmic acceleration.
